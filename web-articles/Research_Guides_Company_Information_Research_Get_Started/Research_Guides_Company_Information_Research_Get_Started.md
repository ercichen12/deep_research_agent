## Welcome!

Use this guide to find important company information resources and learn research tips.

Companies are organizations doing business as corporations, partnerships, or sole proprietorships. They can be categorized as public or private, or a parent or subsidiary/division.

Companies that sell securities to the public and meet certain size thresholds must file disclosure documents with regulatory bodies such as the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC); these companies are said to be publicly-held. Parent companies may own subsidiaries, which are separate legal organizations, and be divided into divisions.

-   [ABI/INFORM Collection (ProQuest)](https://guides.lib.uni.edu/abi-inform-complete) This link opens in a new window

    Journal articles, newspapers, and reports about business, management, and economics.

-   [Business Source Elite (EBSCO)](https://guides.lib.uni.edu/business-source-elite) This link opens in a new window

-   [Nexis Uni](https://guides.lib.uni.edu/nexis-uni) This link opens in a new window

    News, business, legal, medical, and academic resources. News coverage from 1980 to present.

-   [Business Market Research Collection](https://guides.lib.uni.edu/business-market-research-collection) This link opens in a new window

    Reports and news on both public and private companies, including information about their key executives.
